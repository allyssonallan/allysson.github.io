Hi, I am Allysson Allan de Farias. The surname has a portuguese origin.

I have a fluid career path from Humanities to Biological Sciences, I am biologist with a master's degree in archaeology and doctorate in genetics. Postdoctoral researcher at the Università degli Studi di Padova.

Who I am

I was born in Brazil, where I took my undergraduate degree in biology at the State University of Paraiba (2006–2010) with master’s in archaeology at the Federal University of Pernambuco (2011–2013), and a doctoral degree in genetics at the University of Sao Paulo (2014–2018). Currently, I am working close to population medical genetics, collaborating as Senior Bioinformatician at the State University of Paraiba since 2014, who handle genomic data to teach programming languages, and investigate recessive autosomal diseases and the demographic history of admixed populations. Also, I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Università degli Studi di Padova investigating polygenic risk scores in admixed populations.

Which skills I have

As expected for a good researcher, I am resilient. One part of my skills is based on the search for funding agencies, collaborating in many research groups from diverse institutions in Brazil to compete in national and international grants. Another part is inside the scientific career, what enabled me to handle the huge amount of data from biobanks and population genetic databases (What I do); each research question raised from different groups upgraded my programming skills, I am frequently investigating how to interpret the genomic data from Brazilian tri-hybrid population.

What I am looking for

My desire is to unpick how humans have moved and evolved, uncovering some diseases during these processes of change. I am interested in key events in human evolution and prehistory. Also, I would like to draw a picture of the human population diversity in the past, to see how they relate to people living in recently admixed populations. In sum, I am passionate about big questions from human evolution to precision medicine.

What I do

I began my career in Bioinformatics during my undergraduate classes in production engineering at Federal University of Campina Grande (2006) while I was studying biology in parallel. My first language was Fortran much oriented to mathematics. Also, during the engineering course, I practiced C with the same orientation. Then occurred the interruption of the engineering path to exclusively follow the Biology course at the State University of Paraiba, my alma mater. The master’s in archaeology allowed me to begin practicing R language and analyzing spectroscopy data from pre-historical bones from 2011 to 2013. I returned to practice R during the doctorate using microarray data; meanwhile, I was learning Python to handle local ancestry pipelines and data filtering. Several programs to manage vcf and plink files were run with merged data from multiple sources to answer questions related to population genetics. The collaborations during the doctorate allowed me to use the server of Human Genome and Stem-Cell Research Center at the University of Sao Paulo. I am collaborating with a research group from the State University of Paraiba as Senior Bioinformatician. Several questions about GWAS, demographic history, endogamy and personalized medicine have been investigated with genetic data from semi-isolated and poor communities from Northeast Brazilian backlands. Since the beginning of coronavirus pandemic, I have a postdoctoral position in human population genetics at the Università degli Studi di Padova looking for the effects of ancestry in polygenic risk scores.
